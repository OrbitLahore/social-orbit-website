// 01 — why it works
// the infrastructure problem

lahore is full of people worth knowing. the creative who can hold a conversation for three hours. the person with a take on everything. the one who makes a room better just by being in it.

the problem isn't the people. it's the infrastructure. everything is either too big, too transactional, or too much of the same crowd you already know.

social orbit is a room designed to make that happen more often. small enough that everyone actually talks. structured enough that conversation doesn't stay surface level. different enough that you leave thinking about it.

"you meet someone interesting and think —
why haven't i been in a room with this person before?"

// 02 — what we built
// how the room works

meeting people is easy. meeting people you actually want to see again is the hard part. most social setups leave that entirely to luck — awkward silences, phones out, everyone gravitating to who they already know.

we over-engineered the first 20 minutes. the room is alive when you arrive. there's already something happening. you get a job before your phone does. and the activity — different every time — is designed so that a real conversation becomes the path of least resistance.

no "what do you do" openers. no business cards. no one sitting alone. you don't have to try hard. the room does the work.

what it is ✓
  • small on purpose
  • a different activity every time
  • status-neutral from the door
  • designed so you don't sit alone
  • slightly weird. always worth it.
